## Introduction Section

### H2: Expert Crease Repair When Others Say "No"

Not all dents are created equal. While round dings and hail damage respond well to standard PDR techniques, **creases present unique challenges** that require advanced training, specialized tools, and exceptional skill.

A crease is a sharp, linear dent that distorts the metal along a line rather than a point. These often occur from:
- Shopping cart corners
- Bicycle handlebars
- Low-speed impacts with poles or barriers
- Door edges hitting your panel
- Debris kicked up on the highway

At Hail Lions PDR, our Vale-certified technicians specialize in crease repair. We've invested in advanced training and proprietary tools specifically designed to tackle these challenging repairs.

## Understanding Crease Damage

### H2: What Makes Creases Different?

#### The Physics of Creases
Unlike round dents where metal simply depresses, creases create tension along a line. The metal is stretched and work-hardened, making it resistant to standard PDR techniques.

#### Types of Creases We Repair

**Soft Creases**
Gentle lines with minimal stretching. These respond well to advanced PDR and often completely disappear.

**Sharp Creases**
Deep, well-defined lines with significant metal tension. These require specialized tools and techniques but can often be dramatically improved or fully removed.

**Body Line Creases**
Damage that follows or crosses your vehicle's natural body lines. These are particularly challenging as they affect the panel's designed contour.

**Complex Creases**
Creases with multiple angles, intersections, or accompanying dents. Our technicians excel at these multi-faceted repairs.

## Our Advanced Crease Repair Process

### H2: How We Repair Creases

#### Step 1: Comprehensive Analysis
We examine the crease under specialized lighting to understand its depth, length, sharpness, and the extent of metal stretching. This determines our repair approach.

#### Step 2: Tension Release
Before attempting to remove the crease, we often need to release built-up tension in the metal. This may involve:
- Strategic tapping to relax the metal
- Heat application to reduce work-hardening
- Specialized blending techniques

#### Step 3: Precision Tool Selection
Crease repair requires specific tools:
- **Sharp tip tools** for precise pressure points
- **Crease tabs** for glue pulling applications
- **Blending hammers** for surface refinement
- **LED reflection systems** for real-time monitoring

#### Step 4: Gradual Restoration
We work the crease in small sections, gradually massaging the metal back to its original position. This requires patience and an artist's understanding of metal behavior.

#### Step 5: Surface Perfection
After the crease is removed, we refine the surface to ensure perfect reflection and contour. The repair should be completely invisible.

## FAQ Section

### H2: Crease Repair FAQ

#### Can all creases be repaired with PDR?
Most creases can be significantly improved or completely removed with PDR. Success depends on crease depth, sharpness, metal stretching, and paint condition. We provide honest assessments—if PDR won't achieve satisfactory results, we'll tell you.

#### How much does crease repair cost?
Crease repair typically ranges $150-$400 depending on length, depth, and complexity. Long or particularly sharp creases may cost more. We provide free estimates so you know the exact cost before we begin.

#### How long does crease repair take?
Most crease repairs take 1-3 hours. Complex creases or those requiring extensive tension release may take longer. We'll give you a specific timeframe during your assessment.

#### Will the crease be completely invisible?
Our goal is 100% removal, and we achieve this on the majority of creases. Some severe creases may leave very subtle traces visible only under specific lighting angles. We'll discuss expected results before beginning work.

#### Do you repair creases on aluminum panels?
Yes! Many modern vehicles use aluminum hoods, doors, and fenders. Aluminum crease repair requires different techniques than steel, and our technicians are specifically trained and certified in aluminum PDR.

#### What's the difference between a dent and a crease?
A dent is a rounded depression in the metal. A crease is a sharp, linear dent that creates a line of damage. Creases involve metal stretching and tension that make them more challenging to repair than simple dents.

#### Can you repair creases on body lines?
Yes, body line creases are challenging but often repairable. These require exceptional skill as the repair must preserve the panel's designed contour and character lines.

#### Is there a warranty on crease repairs?
Yes, all crease repairs carry our lifetime warranty. If any repaired area shows issues, we'll address it at no charge.
