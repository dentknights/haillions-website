That annoying door ding in your pristine panel doesn't have to mean a trip to the body shop. Whether it's from a runaway shopping cart, a careless driver in the parking lot, or an unknown impact, **paintless dent repair (PDR) offers the perfect solution.**

At Hail Lions PDR, we specialize in removing door dings while preserving your vehicle's factory paint. Our precision techniques gently massage the dent from behind the panel, restoring the original contour without fillers, sanding, or repainting.

## Common Door Ding Causes

### H2: Types of Door Dings We Remove

#### Shopping Cart Impacts
The classic parking lot hazard. Shopping carts leave distinctive round or crescent-shaped dings, typically on doors and fenders. These respond excellently to PDR.

#### Car Door Impacts
When someone opens their door into yours. These often create sharp dings near door edges and can sometimes include minor paint transfer (which we can also address).

#### Unknown Impacts
Mystery dings that appear overnight. Whether from debris, bicycles, or other causes, we can remove them without trace.

#### Minor Collision Dings
Low-speed impacts that dent but don't crease the metal. These are ideal candidates for paintless repair.

#### Motorcycle/Bicycle Dings
Handlebar impacts from two-wheeled vehicles passing too close. Often found on doors and quarter panels.

## The Door Ding Removal Process

### H2: How We Remove Door Dings

#### Step 1: Damage Evaluation
We examine the ding under specialized LED lighting to assess size, depth, and location. Most door dings are excellent PDR candidates, but we'll provide honest feedback if conventional repair is needed.

#### Step 2: Access Strategy
Door dings require careful access planning. We may:
- Remove interior door panels
- Access through window openings
- Use factory access points
- Employ glue pulling techniques for edge dings

#### Step 3: Precision Dent Removal
Using specialized PDR tools, we gently massage the metal from behind, gradually working the ding outward. This requires patience, skill, and an artist's eye for contour.

#### Step 4: Surface Refinement
We polish any minor paint transfer and ensure the panel's reflection is perfect. Your door will look like the ding never happened.

## FAQ Section

### H2: Door Ding Removal FAQ

#### How much does door ding removal cost?
Most single door dings cost $75-$150 to repair. Multiple dings on the same panel may range $150-$300. Factors affecting price include ding size, depth, and panel location. We provide free, no-obligation estimates.

#### How long does door ding repair take?
Most door dings are repaired in 30-60 minutes. Multiple dings or complex access requirements may extend this to 1-2 hours. We can often accommodate same-day appointments.

#### Will the paint be damaged?
No! Paintless dent repair preserves your factory paint. We work from behind the panel, never touching the painted surface (except for final polishing). Your original paint warranty remains intact.

#### Can all door dings be fixed with PDR?
About 90% of door dings can be repaired using PDR. Exceptions include dings with paint damage, very sharp creases, or dings on double-panel sections. We'll assess your specific damage and provide honest recommendations.

#### Do you offer mobile service for door dings?
Yes! Our mobile service brings professional door ding removal to your home, office, or anywhere in Greater Houston. Mobile service is perfect for busy professionals and those with multiple vehicles.

#### Is there a warranty on door ding repairs?
Absolutely. All door ding repairs come with our lifetime warranty. If the ding ever reappears, we'll repair it at no charge.

#### Can you fix dings on aluminum panels?
Yes, we repair door dings on aluminum panels. Many modern vehicles (especially Ford F-150s, Audis, and Teslas) use aluminum doors. Our technicians have specialized training in aluminum PDR.

#### What about dings with paint transfer?
Minor paint transfer often polishes off during our repair process. For more significant paint damage, we can recommend trusted paint touch-up specialists who work with us regularly.
