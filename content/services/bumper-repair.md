## Types of Bumper Damage We Repair

### H2: Bumper Repair Services

#### Plastic Bumper Dents
Modern vehicles use thermoplastic olefin (TPO) bumpers that can often be reshaped. We use heat and specialized PDR techniques to restore the original contour.

While not technically dents, we can often polish out minor scuffs and paint transfer that accompany bumper impacts.

## The Bumper Repair Process

### H2: How We Repair Bumpers

#### Step 1: Damage Assessment
We evaluate the bumper material (plastic type), damage extent, paint condition, and underlying structure. Not all bumper damage can be repaired with PDR, and we'll give you an honest assessment.

#### Step 2: Access Preparation
Depending on the bumper design, we may:
- Remove the bumper cover
- Access from wheel wells
- Work through existing openings
- Use specialized plastic repair tools

#### Step 3: Heat Application
Plastic requires controlled heat to become workable. We use professional heat systems to warm the plastic to the optimal temperature for reshaping.

#### Step 4: Precision Reshaping
Using PDR tools designed for plastic, we gradually massage the dent outward, restoring the original contour without cracking or damaging the material.

#### Step 5: Cooling & Finishing
We carefully cool the plastic to set the new shape, then polish any minor surface imperfections. The result is a bumper that looks like new.

## Bumper Repair vs. Replacement

### H2: Making the Smart Choice

#### When PDR Bumper Repair Makes Sense
- **Minor to moderate dents** without paint damage
- **Cost savings** - Often 50-70% less than replacement
- **OEM preservation** - Keep your original, perfectly-fitted bumper
- **Time savings** - Same-day vs. days at a body shop
- **Environmental benefit** - Reduce waste from discarded bumpers

#### When Replacement May Be Necessary
- **Severe cracks or tears** in the plastic
- **Structural damage** to bumper supports
- **Extensive paint damage** requiring full refinishing
- **Missing pieces** or severe deformation

#### Our Honest Assessment Promise
We'll never recommend PDR if replacement is truly the better option. Our reputation depends on doing right by our customers, not maximizing repair revenue.

## FAQ Section

### H2: Bumper Repair FAQ

#### Can all bumper dents be repaired with PDR?
Many bumper dents can be repaired with PDR, but not all. Success depends on the plastic type, damage severity, and paint condition. During your free assessment, we'll determine if PDR is viable for your specific damage.

#### How much does bumper dent repair cost?
Bumper PDR typically costs $150-$350, significantly less than the $500-$1,500+ for bumper replacement with painting. Exact pricing depends on damage size, location, and bumper type.

#### How long does bumper repair take?
Most bumper dent repairs are completed in 1-2 hours. Complex repairs or those requiring bumper removal may take longer. We can often accommodate same-day service.

#### Will the paint be damaged?
PDR preserves your existing paint. However, bumpers with existing paint damage (chips, cracks, or severe scuffing) may need touch-up work after PDR. We can recommend trusted paint services if needed.

#### Do you repair chrome bumpers?
Chrome bumpers on classic vehicles require different techniques than modern plastic bumpers. We can assess chrome bumper damage and advise on the best repair approach.

#### Can you repair textured bumpers?
Yes! Many trucks and SUVs have textured bumpers. Our techniques can often repair dents while preserving the factory texture pattern.

#### Is there a warranty on bumper repairs?
Yes, all bumper repairs carry our lifetime warranty. If the repaired area shows any issues, we'll address it at no charge.

#### Do you work with insurance on bumper repairs?
If your bumper damage is from a covered incident, we can work with your insurance company. PDR is often the preferred repair method, and we handle claims paperwork.
